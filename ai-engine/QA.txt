// Fees
1. Is the fee paid annually or per semester?
->  At Adamas University, fees are typically paid on a per-semester basis.

2. What is the deadline for fee payment?
->  The fee payment window for the Odd Semester is generally from October 15th to November 15th, and for the Even Semester, it is from March 15th to April 15th.

3. Can I pay the fees in installments?
->  Currently, Adamas University does not offer an installment facility; the semester fees must be paid in full by the stipulated deadline.

4. Are there any additional lab or library fees?
->  No, there are no separate additional charges for lab or library usage, as these facilities are included in the overall academic structure.

5. How can I pay the fees online?
->  Fees can be paid conveniently through the official Adamas University Student Portal using various online payment methods.

6. Is the admission fee refundable if I withdraw?
->  In the event of a withdrawal, a processing fee will be deducted, and the remaining amount will be refunded in accordance with the university’s refund policy.

7. Are there any hidden charges I should know about?
->  No, Adamas University maintains full transparency, and there are no hidden charges beyond the fees mentioned in the official structure.

8. What is the examination fee per semester?
->  The examination fee is already included within the consolidated semester fee, so no separate payment is required for regular end-term exams.

9. What is the late fee penalty?
->  If the fee is paid after the deadline, a late fine of ₹500 is charged for the first day of delay, followed by an additional penalty of ₹250 for each subsequent day.

10. Do international students have a different fee structure?
->  No, the fee structure for international students at Adamas University remains the same as that for domestic students.

11. Does the fee increase every year?
->  Any adjustment to the fee structure is subject to the university's management decisions and will be notified to the students accordingly.

// Admission
1. When does the admission process start?
->  The admission process at Adamas University typically commences in the early months of the calendar year, usually around January or February, for the upcoming academic session.

2. What is the last date to submit the application form?
->  The deadline for submitting application forms varies depending on the specific course and the phase of admissions. We recommend checking the "Admissions" section on the official Adamas University website for the most current dates.

3. What are the eligibility criteria for [Course]?
->  Eligibility criteria depend on the specific program. Generally, for Undergraduate courses, a pass in 10+2 from a recognized board is required, while for Postgraduate courses, a relevant Bachelor's degree is necessary. Specific aggregate percentage requirements vary by school.

4. Is there an entrance exam for admission?
->  Yes, Adamas University conducts the Adamas University Admission Test (AUAT) for several programs. Additionally, scores from national-level exams like JEE Main, GATE, MAT, CAT, or CLAT are also accepted for relevant technical and professional courses.

5. How can I apply online?
->  To apply online, please visit the official Adamas University website (www.adamasuniversity.ac.in), click on the "Apply Now" button, register with your details, fill out the application form, and pay the prescribed application fee.

6. What documents are required for admission?
->  The standard documents required include your 10th and 12th-grade mark sheets and certificates, graduation transcripts (for PG), a valid ID proof (Aadhaar Card), recent passport-size photographs, and a medical fitness certificate.

7. What is the minimum percentage required for [Course]?
->  The minimum percentage required varies; however, most programs require a minimum aggregate of 50% to 60% in your previous qualifying examination. Professional courses may have higher benchmarks.

8. Can I apply for more than one course?
->  Yes, you are permitted to apply for more than one course at Adamas University; however, you will need to submit separate application forms and fees for each program.

9. What should I do if my payment failed during registration?
->  If a payment fails, please wait for 24 hours to see if the status updates. If the amount was debited but the portal shows "Failed," you should contact the Adamas University Admission Helpdesk or the Accounts Department with your transaction ID for assistance.
